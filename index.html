ONCE UPON A TIME – long, long ago – there was a queen who in winter was sitting by the open window embroidering. 'Ouch!' she cried suddenly, for

she had pricked her finger. A drop of blood fell on the snow under the window. 'I wish, I wish,' said the queen, 'that I had a daughter with a skin as white as this snow, with hair as black as the ebony of this window-frame, and with lips as red as this drop of blood.' And what do you think? After a while a daughter was

born, and the girl did indeed have hair as black as ebony, a skin as white as snow, and lips as red as blood. So the king and queen were quite happy, you understand, and they called their daughter Snow White.

 

 

But one day the queen became ill, even so badly that she died. The king, who did not like it, so alone, after a while married again.

His new wife was very beautiful, but what the king did not know was that she thought of nothing else all day long but being beautiful. In her room she even had a magic mirror for that purpose.

 

Every morning she would stand in front of this mirror and ask:

 

Mirror, mirror on the wall, who is the fairest of them all?

 

And because it was a magic mirror, he could talk, and he always answered:

 

You are the most beautiful in the land .

 

 

The queen then sat down contentedly on a chair in front of her dressing table to  make herself even more beautiful.

But one day things went differently. The queen stood in front of the mirror again and asked:

 

 

Mirror, mirror on the wall, who is the fairest of them all?

And do you know what she heard now?

 

Oh, Queen, you are the most beautiful in this room,

but Snow White is a thousand times more beautiful than you.

 

The queen could not believe her ears. She pushed aside the curtain and saw Snow White playing, and knew at once that the mirror was right. 'No one must be more beautiful than I,' the queen growled. And then she thought of something very evil.

 

She called the palace hunter to her and gave him a large knife. 'Tomorrow you will take Snow White into the forest,' she said, 'and there you will stab her to death with this knife.' Then she gave him a small box.

"And to make sure she's dead, I want this box back with Snow White's heart in it."

 

 

The next day the huntsman went with Snow White into the forest. But when he took out his knife, he felt sorry for her. 'Go away, Snow White,' he said, 'as far away as possible, because the queen wants me to kill you.' Frightened, Snow White ran as fast as she could, deep into the forest. And do you know what the huntsman did? He killed a wild boar and put its heart in the queen's box.

 

 

Meanwhile, Snow White kept running. She ran over seven mountains until, exhausted, she came to a tiny house. The front door was not locked.

Snow White had to duck when she went inside.

There must be seven children living here, she thought. Look, there are seven little chairs, seven little plates, seven little glasses and seven little spoons. She

walked up a small narrow staircase and at the top she saw seven little beds in a row. Suddenly she felt how tired she was. (Yes, what do you expect, when you have run so hard over seven mountains!) And before she knew it she was stretched out across three beds and fell into a deep sleep.

 

Because of that she didn't hear the residents coming home. They weren't seven

children but seven dwarfs, and they saw right away that someone had been in the house. They crept upstairs and discovered the sleeping Snow White.


"Oh, how sweet she is," said one dwarf. "And how beautiful," said another.

"Shhh, don't wake them!" said a third. And they quietly crept back down the stairs.

 

 

 

After a long time Snow White woke up and when she found the seven dwarfs downstairs, she told them what had happened to her. The dwarfs had heard of the evil queen. 'They say she can do magic,' they whispered and crept closer together.

 

The next morning the dwarves had to go to the silver mine to work as usual. At the door they said to Snow White: 'Be careful, don't let anyone in. You never know!' They waved to her and disappeared behind the mountains.

 

 

 

Meanwhile, in the palace, the hunter had given the box to the evil queen.

 

"Now I'm the most beautiful again," she said with satisfaction and quickly went to stand in front of the magic mirror.

 

Mirror, mirror on the wall, who is the fairest of them all?

 

The mirror replied:

 

Oh, Queen, you are the most beautiful in this room,

but Snow White is a thousand times more beautiful than you.

 

The queen showed the box to the mirror. 'Ha! That can't be!

Snow White is dead. Here is her heart!' But the mirror answered:

 

Oh, Queen, the fairest in this room are you, but Snow White beyond the Seven Mountains in the house of the

Seven Dwarfs is a thousand times fairer than you!

 

The queen understood that the huntsman had fooled her. She trembled with rage.


“Then I'll kill her myself,” she said, gritting her teeth.

 

She went down six flights of stairs to the cellar and there conjured up a poisoned apple. It was red on one side and green on the other. She changed herself into an old woman and set off, over the seven mountains, to the house of the seven dwarves.

"I'll get her!" she growled again and again. And in her basket, among the other apples, lay the poisoned apple... Snow White, who was left alone in

the cottage of the seven dwarfs, was just about to put a pan of soup on the fire, when there was a tap at the window. It was an old woman, who was carrying a basket of apples.

 

'May I rest here for a while? I am so tired,' she panted. Snow White hesitated. She opened the window and said, 'I am sorry, old woman, the dwarves will not let anyone in. But I can give you a glass of water.' When the witch had

finished the glass, she said, 'Since you have been so kind to me, I will give you an apple. Look, a very beautiful red-green apple. Take a nice

bite!' Snow White took a bite and… fell dead on the ground.

 

 

 

'Hahahaha,' laughed the wicked queen (for that is what it was of course!), 'hahahaha! Now I am the fairest of them all again!' And she hurried back to her

palace, on the other side of the seven mountains.

When the dwarfs came home they found Snow White lying dead on the floor. The dwarfs were deeply and deeply sad, so sad that they could not say goodbye to her. They made a beautiful glass coffin for her and put her in it, so that they could always look at her.

 

A few days later a prince happened to be riding through the forest on his horse and saw the glass coffin. He thought Snow White was so beautiful that he lifted the glass lid and gave her a kiss.

 

Apparently Snow White was not completely dead, because she started coughing in fright and the poisoned piece of apple shot out of her throat.

She sat up in surprise and asked, "Where am I? What happened?"


“Hurray!” shouted the dwarves.

How happy they were that Snow White was alive again! The prince was too. He took Snow White on his horse and they rode off together to his palace.

There they got married and lived happily ever after.

 

And the evil queen? She heard from the mirror that Snow White was still the most beautiful. She found that so terrible that she dropped dead.

Yes, really, she fell over in sheer rage, just like that, right there in front of the mirror.

The princess looked into the depths. What could she do but promise, for she couldn’t swim...

At home, the king embraced his daughter. He was overjoyed to see her alive and said, “I said that your savior can marry you. The wedding is the day after tomorrow.” And he patted the driver approvingly on the shoulder. The princess could, of course, not say that the driver hadn’t saved her at all, because she had promised, and a princess always keeps her promises. But she did come up with a trick.

“I’m still too young to marry, father,” she said. “Let’s postpone it for a year.”

The driver naturally didn’t want to hear of it, but the king thought his daughter was still a bit too young and postponed the wedding.

---

After a year, the princess said, “Father, I still feel too young to marry. Let’s postpone it for another year.” The driver was beside himself with anger, but the king agreed with his daughter.

The next year, the princess said for the third time that she was too young to marry. But now the king said, “That’s fine. We’ll postpone it for one more year, but then I don’t want to hear of any more postponements. Because I promised your savior.” Yes... and then the next year came. And yes... now the princess had to marry. On the appointed day, there was a celebration throughout the land. The bells rang, the people danced in the streets, and everyone was joyful. Except for the princess, of course. She sat sadly next to the driver at the feast. She could only hope that the boy with the dogs would keep his word. After all, he was supposed to return after three years, right?

She did not know that he had just arrived in the city at that moment. He saw all those happy people and asked why there was a celebration.

“Three years ago, the princess was rescued from the dragon by a driver,” was the answer, “and today they are getting married.”

“That man is a liar!” cried the boy. “I saved her!” But the people didn’t believe him and got angry. The guards even had to get involved because it was forbidden to quarrel on this festive day.

The boy was thrown into prison along with his dogs. When they were alone, the boy said to Mustard, “Mustard, bite through the bars!”

---

That was done in no time. After some gnawing and cracking, the boy sneaked out of the prison, and the dogs followed him. He hadn’t eaten anything since that morning and was now very hungry. “Salt,” he whispered, “go get something to eat for me!” And off went Salt.

---

The dog went into the royal banquet hall and grabbed a roasted chicken from the royal table. The princess recognized the dog, jumped up, and ran after him.

She found the boy a street away and took him back to the palace. “Father,” she said, “this is my savior. Not that stupid driver.” And then she told everything.

---

The wedding went ahead, but now with the boy as the groom instead of the driver.

And what happened to the driver? He went to prison. And the dogs? They suddenly disappeared without a trace, and no one knew where they had gone.

LONG, LONG AGO, in a land very far away, there was a rich man who was called Bluebeard. His hair was just black on his head, and his mustache and thick eyebrows were pitch black too, but his beard was truly completely blue. He lived far away in a dark castle that had more than seventy rooms and was thus far too large for just him. That was also the reason why he was looking for a wife, or rather: he was looking for a wife again, for he had been married a few times before. What had happened to those women, nobody knew...

---

So when he once saw a beautiful girl walking, he visited her father and asked if he could marry the girl. The father thought, If this rich man marries my daughter, perhaps I will get some of his money and become rich myself. Well, he was very much in favor of it, so he didn’t hesitate for a second and said immediately: 'Yes!' He didn’t even ask his daughter what she thought! Maybe on purpose, because he could clearly see that she was a bit scared of Bluebeard. And that wasn’t so strange, for Bluebeard had eerie eyes and a bad temper.

---

No one had ever seen him laugh, and then there was of course that strange blue beard...

The wedding was celebrated, but before the girl left for the castle with the seventy rooms, she quickly whispered to her three brothers: 'If you hear me screaming for help, come to me immediately!' The brothers promised this, and with a knot in her stomach, the girl got into the carriage. It was three days of driving through dense forests and misty swamps, and then the girl found herself alone with Bluebeard in the big gloomy castle, far away from everything and everyone.

---

A month later, Bluebeard unexpectedly had to go on a journey. He gave her a large bunch of keys. 'Here are the keys to all the rooms,' he said. 'You may go anywhere, except for one room. This little key is for a room in the cellar, and that's the only space you must not enter. The door is red, so you can't be mistaken. If you do look in that room, I will punish you severely.' After these words, Bluebeard mounted his horse and left.

The girl was curious and immediately tried all the keys. She didn't know that there were so many kinds of rooms – one had red wallpaper, another green, the third had a marble floor, and the fourth had expensive tapestries on the walls. Every day she saw new rooms, walked through new hallways. Up and down the stairs she went, and suddenly, before she realized it, she found herself in the cellar in front of the forbidden red door...

It was not a big door; in fact, it was quite an ordinary door. She felt the rough wood and the large black hinges. How strange it was that she wasn't allowed in there. That evening she couldn't fall asleep. She kept thinking about that room. What was in there? Why wasn't she allowed to know?

Fortunately, her sister Anna came to stay, so she could think of something else, and together the sisters wandered through the castle. They looked at all the rooms, but the girl said nothing about the room in the cellar.

---

On the day before Bluebeard was to return home, she could no longer suppress her curiosity. When her sister Anna took an afternoon nap, she grabbed the little key and tiptoed down the stairs to the cellar. For quite some time she stood with her heart pounding in front of the red door, the little key trembling in her hand. I'm just going to push against it a little, the girl thought, maybe it’s not even locked. But the door was locked...

I'm just going to try if the key fits, the girl thought, you never know, maybe it’s the wrong key. But the key fit perfectly... And if I just turn it a little... She held her breath as the door slowly and creakingly opened.

---

It was quite dark inside. She took a step into the room and screamed in fright. There were all coffins! The girl wanted to quickly leave the room but dropped the key. She quickly picked it up, and in an instant, she had closed the door behind her and locked it.

 

---

 

Back in her bedroom, she saw to her horror that there was blood on the key. No matter how much she scrubbed and cleaned with soap and a steel brush, it wouldn’t come off! She wrapped it in a handkerchief and hid it far away in a deep drawer. Tomorrow Bluebeard would come home, and what if he asked for the little key...

 

---

 

The girl didn't know what to do! All night long she walked back and forth, back and forth through the room.
Early the next morning she heard the outer gate open. Heavy steps sounded in the hallway and a moment later he was standing in front of her. The girl was so scared that she immediately burst into tears. Bluebeard understood immediately what was going on. The bunch of keys was on the table and the small key was not there… ‘You have been in the cellar,’ he said, ‘so now I must punish you. I warned you!’ He drew his sword and was about to stab her to death, but the girl dropped to her knees and begged to be allowed to pray in her room. ‘Two minutes and not a second longer,’ said Bluebeard. The girl ran up the stairs – not to her own room, but to her sister
Anna's room. There she called out of the window: ‘Oh dear, dear brothers, where are you? Help me, help me!’ The brothers heard this, who happened to be riding their horses nearby. They immediately turned around and galloped to the castle.

----

Meanwhile, the girl said to her sister, “Quickly, sister Anna, climb to the highest tower and see if you can see them coming!” Anna gathered her skirts and ran up the winding staircase. The girl herself stayed in the room and quickly slid the bolt across the door.

---

“The two minutes are almost up!” Bluebeard called impatiently from below. The girl shouted anxiously upward, “Sister Anna, sister Anna, do you see anything coming?”

And from high up in the tower, Anna called down, “I see nothing, only trees and a dark sky.”

“The two minutes are over!” roared Bluebeard.

 

----

 

‘Oh Anna, Sister Anna, do you see nothing coming?’ the girl cried again in a trembling voice.
‘No, no,’ Anna cried back, ‘I see only mountains and black clouds.’
‘Come down now, or I will come and fetch you!’ shouted Bluebeard.
‘Anna, oh Anna, do you still see nothing coming?’ the girl cried now, weeping.
‘Yes, yes,’ Anna cried cheeringly, ‘I see a cloud of dust, which is fast approaching.’
‘Now I will come and fetch you,’ roared Bluebeard, and he stormed up the stairs with his sword.
He cut a hole in the door and squeezed his way in. In three steps he was at the girl and with both hands he raised his sword, ready to stab her to death.

 

---

At that moment the three brothers burst in and stabbed Bluebeard to death. The girl threw her arms around her brothers, crying. She was saved! That was what you call the nick of time. The girl was now very rich, but she would not continue to live in the castle for anything. She bought another castle (one without a cellar), which may not have been as big, but much more pleasant.

 

---

You surely understand now who lay in those coffins: the girls Bluebeard had married before, all of whom had been just as curious as the girl in this story! So it is a miracle that this story ends so well. But the one who truly deserved a good beating was the girl's father.
